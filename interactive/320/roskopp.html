Jim Phillips

Model: Rob Roskopp I
Manufacturer: Santa Cruz
Year: 1984

In the 1980s, Jim Phillips created the first pro-model for skater Rob Roskopp. The graphic on the board was inspired by Roskopp’s interest in bull’s-eyes and his appreciation of the illustrations of Ed “Big Daddy” Roth, an artist who commonly depicted monsters driving hotrods.

Over time, this graphic – which depicted an arm punching through a bull’s-eye and pointing at Roskopp’s name – gave rise to an evolving series of images. The Roskopp boards were the first series boards in the history of skateboarding. Each time a new graphic in the series was released, it built upon the previous image to continue the visual story in which the monster emerges from the bull’s-eye and reveals itself to be a large, grotesque monster. The Roskopp Bull’s-Eye series is one of the most highly sought sets of skateboards by collectors. Only one full set of unblemished decks is known to exist.
