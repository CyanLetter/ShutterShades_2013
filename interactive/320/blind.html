Marc McKee

Model: Jason Lee
Manufacturer: Blind
Year: 1991

In the early 1990s, a war broke out between small skateboard companies and larger corporate empires like Powell-Peralta. The instigator was Steve Rocco, who founded World Industries in 1987 – one of the first truly “skater-owned” skateboard companies – as a direct challenge to corporate domination of the industry.

Rocco broke all the corporate rules: he paid skaters double the standard royalty rate, he moved away from skull and gore graphics, often putting shocking and uncensored images on decks, and he allowed his designers to appropriate corporate logos with complete disregard for intellectual property rights. “We were just pushing the boundaries of things that hadn’t been done before,” Rocco said. “And why not? I actually felt like we almost had an obligation to do it.”

Rocco directly attacked Powell-Peralta by having artist Marc McKee design a series of decks with graphics that spoofed the larger company’s iconic images. The first in the spoof series was the Dodo Skull graphic, an insulting invention upon the classic Screaming Hawk Skull that skater Tony Hawk made famous. The Dodo Skull deck was designed for rookie skater Jason Lee, who went on to become an actor, star of the TV series My Name is Earl.

McKee also spoofed other well-known images such as Powell-Peralta’s trademark “Ripper” image, the “Skull and Sword” graphic on Ray “Bones” Rodriguez’s pro models, and the “Nordic Skull” image on Per Welinder’s decks. The battle was not limited to skate deck graphics, but extended into advertising as well. On behalf of World Industries and its subsidiaries, Rocco published incendiary ads mocking Powell-Peralta. George Powell, owner of Powell-Peralta, responded by running ads that took aim at the small skater-owned companies, such as this one from 1991, a farce which depicts three Powell-Peralta pro skaters as entrepreneurs and has copy that reads:

LANCE MOUNTAIN, TONY HAWK AND RAY BARBEE PROUDLY ANNOUNCE THE DEBUT OF THEIR 3 NEWEST COMPANIES SCUMBUCKET, BADPERSON AND MECO. RUN OUT QUICK AND BUY BUY BUY BEFORE THESE NEW COMPANIES GO BYE BYE….

Rocco countered with a now infamous advertisement for World Industries subsidiary Blind. Addressing George Powell directly, the ad read, “Dear George,” at the top, depicted skaters displaying the mocking graphics on the spoof decks, and was accompanied by an open letter to George Powell that reads:

After seeing all the ads lately (especially yours), making fun of new or small companies, I realized how stupid I was for having one….Anyway I talked to Jason and Rudy, and we all agreed that we should try to be like you guys.

Rocco’s efforts provoked a drastic decline in the sales of Powell-Peralta decks, while those of smaller skater-owned companies rose in similar proportion, forever changing the face of the skateboarding industry.
