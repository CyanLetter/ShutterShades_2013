Jim Phillips

Pro skater Corey O’Brien wanted a board with graphics that were substantially different from other work created by Santa Cruz’s graphic artist, Jim Phillips, so he went out looking for inspiration and ideas. In doing so, he visited a tattoo shop where he came across the image depicting a reaper holding a crystal ball. He brought the image to Jim Phillips, who suggested changing the crystal ball to a ball of flames.

The subliminal images in the hazy background behind the reaper and the font in which O’Brien’s name is written on the board were inspired by music and images that the skater shared with the designer, including a book illustrated by American pulp fantasy, science fiction, and horror illustrator Virgil Finlay, and Hell Comes To Your House, an American deathrock and punk album.

This board became extremely popular, so much so that Santa Cruz manufactured 10-15,000 per month. Despite this phenomenal popularity and despite the fact that O’Brien received $1 for each board sold, the skater eventually tired of the graphic and moved on to others, though none of his subsequent boards ever came close to topping the Reaper’s sales records.
