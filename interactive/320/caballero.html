Vernon Courtland Johnson (VCJ)

Model: Steve Caballero
Manufacturer: Powell-Peralta
Year: 1981

The artist Vernon Courtland Johnson (VCJ) went to work for the Powell-Peralta skateboard company in the late 1970s and is responsible for many of their iconic graphics, including the Caballero Dragon seen on this board.

Designed for pro skater Steve Caballero—also known as “Cab,”—the dragon recalls the fact that Steve was born in 1964, the Chinese Year of the Dragon. In creating the dragon image, VCJ was inspired by comic book ink effects, in particular those seen in Heavy Metal Comics.

Since its initial release in 1981, there have been numerous inventions on the Caballero Dragon, some of which were made by artist Sean Cliver when he took over VCJ’s position at Powell-Peralta from 1989-1991. As such, The Caballero Dragon is one of the few graphic themes that has transitioned from one artist to another with great success.
