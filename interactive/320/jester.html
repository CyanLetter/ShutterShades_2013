John Lucero

Model: John Lucero Pro Model
Manufacturer: Madrid
Year: 1985

While skating for Madrid, John Lucero found inspiration for his Jester decks in punk music and in his affinity for demonic looking joker figures.

Lucero also loved the 1980s trend for neon colors—he frequently wore fluorescent socks—and he wanted his “Jester” deck to have a florescent pink background. However, because a wash was being used to create the background color, the manufacturer had difficulty in producing a pink color that was sufficiently saturated to appear bright and neon, so the board was first released with a neon green background, and then a few runs were made in lavender.

After the release of the first Jester board, Lucero left Madrid to skate for Schmitt Stix, where he created his own graphics and graphics for other skaters. He continued working with the Jester figure, designing three more Jester decks, all of which were inspired by a dungeon sticker that Lucero got from a box of Trix breakfast cereal when he was a child.

The first of the decks depicts cage bars, the second shows the Jester looking out from behind the cage bars, the third shows the Jester’s head breaking out of the bars, and the fourth shows the Jester free of the bars but with an eight-ball chained to his leg.
